Long Story Short

I helped turn UH Energy content into clearer learning pathways

The UH Energy website needed stronger information architecture for students, professionals, and partners. I focused on making certification pages easier to understand, improving page structure, and creating a more consistent discovery path.

Web Strategy

How might we make dense energy education content easier to scan, compare, and act on?

Content Research

To improve discovery, I assessed how users move through dense program information

I reviewed certification pages, content hierarchy, and navigation friction. The strongest insight was that users needed faster comparison cues and clearer next steps before they could confidently engage.

Solution

I built a cleaner pattern for program discovery

The revised direction emphasized scannable sections, stronger hierarchy, and consistent calls to action so energy education content felt less dense and easier to explore.

Impact

3,000+ Users

Designed with a large prospective learner audience in mind.

21+ Pages

Supported a repeatable certification-page improvement pattern.

Clearer Paths

Strengthened navigation between research, certificates, and learning outcomes.

Reflection

What I learned

This project showed me how product thinking applies to content-heavy websites: clarity, hierarchy, and the next action matter as much as visual polish.
