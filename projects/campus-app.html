Long Story Short

I shaped an AI accessibility concept that helps learners find the right coding support faster

AdaptAI was built around a simple problem: coding resources are often written for a generic learner, which can leave students with disabilities sorting through tools, documentation, and setup advice that may not match their needs. I focused the product around intake, personalization, and clear next-step recommendations.

How might we help learners with disabilities find coding support that matches their specific access needs?

Accessibility Research

To reduce friction, I focused the concept around tailored accessibility support

I mapped where learners can get stuck when coding tools, documentation, and setup instructions are not designed around their access needs. AdaptAI reframes support as a guided matching system that recommends clearer resources and next steps.

Solution

I turned a broad accessibility problem into a focused AI guidance workflow

The final concept included a lightweight intake flow, an accessibility-needs profile, AI-assisted coding resource recommendations, and a guided action plan. The goal was not to replace human support, but to help users arrive at better questions, better tools, and more relevant help faster.

Process

What I would expand next

The next iteration would add user testing with students who rely on assistive technologies, stronger recommendation criteria, and clearer escalation paths to campus resources, mentors, or accessibility offices when AI guidance is not enough.
