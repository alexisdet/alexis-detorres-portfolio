Long Story Short

I turned a messy product operations problem into a clearer execution system

Divide&Conquer is a strategy case about making complex workstreams easier to act on. I focused on separating responsibilities, sequencing priorities, and creating a framework that helps teams move from ambiguity to action.

How might we break an overwhelming workflow into clear owners, priorities, and next steps?

Process Research

To create momentum, I studied where the workflow lost clarity

I grouped recurring blockers, identified unclear ownership points, and looked for the highest-value places to reduce ambiguity. The goal was to make the work legible enough for collaborators to act.

Solution

I created a divide-and-prioritize framework

The framework clarified decision points, reduced ambiguity, and helped the team move from scattered tasks to focused execution with clearer ownership and sequencing.

Impact

Clearer Ownership

Made responsibilities easier to understand and act on.

Better Sequencing

Separated urgent work from dependent future steps.

Less Ambiguity

Improved visibility into what mattered most.

Reflection

What I learned

Good product work often starts by making the messy middle legible. This case helped me practice turning ambiguity into a structure people can actually use.
