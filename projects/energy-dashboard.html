Long Story Short

I created an AI-assisted workflow that turns messy clinical notes into reviewable decisions

AuthForm helps clinics move from unstructured patient notes to payer-ready authorization materials through extraction, criteria review, risk breakdowns, and editable outputs. The experience keeps physicians in control while making status clearer for patients.

How might we help clinics prepare stronger prior authorizations without leaving patients in the dark?

Workflow Research

To reduce delay risk, I mapped the information clinics need before submission

The test case centered on ADHD medication approval, step-therapy evidence, diagnosis documentation, safety screening, and follow-up planning. The biggest opportunity was surfacing missing evidence before submission instead of after denial.

Solution

I designed the product around review, not replacement

AuthForm can extract information, draft rationale, identify missing documentation, and suggest stronger submission paths, but physicians still review, edit, and approve before anything is finalized.

Impact

Faster Case Prep

Clinical notes become structured authorization inputs.

Fewer Gaps

Missing requirements are surfaced before submission.

Safer Follow-Through

Patient guidance supports care beyond approval.

Reflection

What I learned

This project taught me that high-trust AI should support judgment instead of replacing it. The product value came from making complex clinical work easier to review.
