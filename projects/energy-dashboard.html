AuthForm

Created an AI-assisted prior authorization workflow for physicians and a clearer treatment status portal for patients.

Role: Product Strategy, UX/UI Design, AI Workflow Design Timeline: Hackathon Sprint Tools: Base44, Clinical UX, Workflow Design, Prompt Design

The Short Version

I designed a smarter way for clinics to prepare prior authorization requests without leaving patients in the dark. AuthForm helps clinics move from messy patient notes to payer-ready submissions using AI extraction, criteria review, denial-risk breakdowns, and editable authorization materials.

Problem

Prior authorization is not just paperwork. It can delay care. Physicians manage insurer rules, clinical documentation, safety checks, step-therapy requirements, and appeal workflows while patients often cannot see what is happening or what comes next.

Goal

Help clinics reduce preventable prior authorization delays while keeping physicians in control and giving patients plain-language visibility into treatment status.

Case Context

The test case focused on Elena Ramirez, a 29-year-old patient diagnosed with ADHD, Combined Type, with ICD-10 code F90.2. Her physician requested Vyvanse after two prior medication trials, requiring step-therapy evidence, safety screening, documentation of impairment, and a follow-up plan.

Physician Portal

Patient Intake

Physicians can paste a flexible patient summary instead of starting with manual field-by-field entry.

Auto-Extraction Review

AI extracts diagnosis, ICD-10 code, medication, prior treatments, symptoms, side effects, and red flags for review.

Insurer Criteria

The portal maps the case against payer rules, showing what is met, not met, or risky before submission.

AuthForm workflow: intake → extraction → criteria review → risk breakdown → PA output

Key Product Decision

AI assists. Physicians approve. AuthForm can extract information, draft clinical rationale, identify missing documentation, and suggest stronger submission paths, but a physician or clinic staff member still reviews, edits, and approves the output before anything is finalized.

Risk Review

Instead of showing a vague approval score, AuthForm breaks denial risk into documentation completeness, step-therapy compliance, ICD-10 accuracy, safety screening, and cost risk. The score is decision support, not a guarantee.

Patient Portal

Medication Overview

Explains purpose, dosage, common side effects, safety warnings, and previously tried medications.

Status Tracker

Shows the authorization process in plain language so patients know what is pending and what happens next.

Safety & Disposal

Gives safe disposal options, controlled-substance reminders, and patient-friendly medication resources.

Impact

Faster Case Preparation

Clinical notes become structured authorization inputs.

Fewer Gaps

Missing documents and insurer requirements are surfaced earlier.

Clearer Updates

Patients can track status without seeing internal clinical scoring.

Safer Follow-Through

Medication safety guidance supports care beyond approval.

Reflection

This project taught me how to design AI for a high-trust workflow. The strongest AI use case was not replacing physicians. It was reducing repetitive work while keeping review, judgment, and approval in human hands.
