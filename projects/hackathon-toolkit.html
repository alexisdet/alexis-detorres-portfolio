Long Story Short

I defined the product roadmap from student pain points and shipped an AI-powered syllabus platform in 48 hours

Mentora, originally developed as DEMI, helps students manage coursework by converting syllabus information into a clearer academic planning workflow. I synthesized repeated pain points from student interviews and prioritized assignment tracking, calendar integration, and personalized study planning.

How might we help students move from passive syllabus reading to active academic planning?

Student Research

To prioritize the roadmap, I synthesized insights from 10+ student interviews

Students repeatedly struggled with assignment tracking, calendar integration, and understanding how coursework should translate into weekly study plans. Those insights shaped features that helped drive adoption by 40+ users during the project cycle.

Solution

I scoped AI around the academic planning workflow

The final product included AI-powered syllabus analysis, auto-generated class pages, assignment tracking, calendar integration, personalized study plans, a course-specific DEMI assistant, a grade calculator, and final grade forecasting.

Process

I kept the product focused by prioritizing the repeated student pain points first

Because the build window was only 48 hours, I separated nice-to-have academic features from the core workflow students needed immediately: upload a syllabus, understand upcoming work, plan around deadlines, and know what to do next. That constraint helped the product stay practical instead of becoming a broad AI school assistant.

01

Interview Themes

Students wanted fewer scattered class documents and clearer deadline visibility.

02

Feature Cut

Prioritized planning and tracking before lower-value personalization ideas.

03

Usability Goal

Made the output feel like a weekly action plan instead of another document to read.

Reflection

What I learned

This project strengthened my ability to use student research to prioritize features under a tight timeline. It also helped me understand how to scope AI around a real workflow instead of treating AI as the entire product.
