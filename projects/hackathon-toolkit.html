Long Story Short

I designed a new way for students to plan a semester from the documents they already have

Mentora turns syllabi, grade rules, deadlines, and LMS context into one organized academic command center. Instead of asking students to manually rebuild their semester, the product extracts key information and makes it easier to plan, prioritize, and ask course-specific questions.

How might we help students turn scattered academic information into a clear semester plan?

Student Research

To create a useful academic product, I mapped where students lose time and confidence

I focused on students balancing coursework, work, leadership, and shifting deadlines. The key gap was not motivation. It was fragmentation: students had the information, but it lived across too many disconnected places.

Solution

I translated syllabus chaos into a guided planning system

The final concept starts with syllabus upload, then creates synced classes, deadlines, grade tools, and AI context. The workflow keeps students oriented by showing what needs attention next and why it matters.

Impact

Less Manual Setup

Students can upload syllabi instead of copying every deadline by hand.

Clearer Priorities

Tasks, grades, and calendar context make the next step easier to choose.

Faster Answers

Demi gives students a lower-friction way to ask course-specific questions.

Reflection

What I learned

This project pushed me to design the full product system, not just screens. The strongest idea was connecting academic planning, grade clarity, and AI support into one coherent workflow.
