Mentora

Academic planning that helps students turn static syllabi into assignment tracking, calendar planning, study support, and grade forecasting.

Timeline48-hour build
TeamHackathon Team
RoleProduct Direction, UX/UI Design

Long Story Short

I led product direction for an academic planning platform built around real student workflows

Mentora helps students manage coursework by converting syllabus information into a clearer academic planning workflow. DEMI functions as the course-specific AI assistant inside the platform, helping students ask questions about their classes without making AI the entire product.

01

Assignment Tracking

Prioritized turning syllabus content into actionable deadlines and calendar-based planning.

02

Grade Planning

Separated current grade awareness from future final-grade forecasting.

03

DEMI Assistant

Positioned DEMI as a course-specific support feature inside a broader planning platform.

How might we help students move from passive syllabus reading to active academic planning?

Student Research

To prioritize the roadmap, I synthesized insights from 10+ student interviews

Students repeatedly struggled with assignment tracking, calendar integration, and understanding grade impact. Those insights shaped features that helped students plan their week, track academic progress, and understand what actions would affect their final grade.

Solution

I scoped AI around the academic planning workflow

The final product included syllabus upload, auto-generated class pages, assignment tracking, calendar integration, personalized study plans, a course-specific DEMI assistant, a grade calculator, and a final grade forecast.

Impact

48 Hours

Built under a tight hackathon timeline.

10+ Interviews

Research shaped feature prioritization.

40+ Users

Adopted by early users during the project cycle.

Reflection

What I learned

This project strengthened my ability to use student research to prioritize features under a tight timeline. It also helped me understand how to scope AI around a real workflow instead of treating AI as the entire product.
